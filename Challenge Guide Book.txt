Squad Hackathon 3.0 goes beyond building to building with purpose.
The theme “Smart Systems: The Intelligent Economy” is a call to
the boldest, most curious minds in Nigerian universities to engineer
solutions that don’t just process payments, but reshape how
communities interact with money.
The challenges are grounded in a single belief: financial inclusion is not
a charity problem. It is a technology problem. And you have the tools to
solve it.
Every challenge requires integration with Squad’s API stack. Every
solution must demonstrate real intelligence whether through AI
automation, predictive data, or bold financial innovation.
Building a demo is not enough. Build something that works for the
people who need it most.

“Proof of Life”
Build an AI solution that helps people, businesses, or institutions verify,
trust, or authenticate something in a context where fraud or opacity is
common.
Fake exam results. Ghost workers on government payrolls. Counterfeit
products in supply chains. Unverified vendors collecting payments.
Misinformation spreading as fact.
The AI must do the verification or trust-scoring work, forcing teams into
computer vision, NLP, or anomaly detection territory. This is not a chat
interface challenge.

04
Every team submits the same way. No exceptions, no extensions.
Here is exactly what you need to bring on the day.
1. Product Demo (Live or Recorded)
A working demo of your solution, live is preferred, but a recorded
walkthrough is accepted if connectivity is a concern. The demo
should show the core user journey end-to-end, including Squad API
integration in action. You have 5 minutes. Make them count.
2. Pitch Deck (10 Slides Max)
Your deck should cover exactly this, in this order:
Slide Content
1 Problem — Who is suffering and why does it matter right now?
2 Your Target User — Be specific. Name them, describe them, show you
have spoken to them
3 Solution Overview — What did you build and how does it work?
4 Squad API Integration — Which APIs did you use and how are they
central to your solution?
5 AI / Data Intelligence — What pillar(s) does your solution address and
how?
6 User Flow — Walk us through the product experience step by step
7 Impact Potential — Who can this reach and how quickly?
8 Scalability & Business Model — How does this grow and sustain itself?
3. GitHub Repository
A clean, documented codebase submitted via a public GitHub link
before your presentation window closes. Undocumented or empty
repositories will be flagged with judges.
4. One-Pager Summary
A single-page PDF (A4) summarising your solution, the problem it
solves, the Squad APIs used, and which of the four pillars your solution
addresses. This is left with judges after your presentation. Keep it
sharp.
5. Q&A (5 Minutes)
Judges will ask questions immediately following your demo and pitch.
Every team member should be ready to speak. The strongest teams
know their product, their user, and their numbers cold.
7 Research & Validation — What evidence supports your assumptions?
8 The Team — Who built this and why are you the right people to take it
